## 3. Your responsibility as a user

JustHireMe is a tool that retrieves and processes third-party content (such as job postings) that you direct it to. **You** are responsible for ensuring your use does not infringe third-party rights, including:

- complying with the terms of service and `robots` rules of any site you scrape or query;
- not redistributing scraped third-party content unlawfully;
- not using generated materials to copy, plagiarize, or misrepresent another person's work or identity.

JustHireMe does not host a public library of third-party job content; leads are stored locally on your device.

## 4. Reporting alleged infringement (takedown notice)

If you believe content **in this repository, in the Software's bundled assets, or on the Site** infringes your copyright or other IP rights, send a written notice to our designated contact (Section 7) including all of the following:

1. Your physical or electronic **signature** (your full legal name typed is acceptable for electronic notices).
2. **Identification of the copyrighted work** or other IP right you claim is infringed (or a representative list).
3. **Identification of the allegedly infringing material** and information reasonably sufficient to locate it — for code, a file path, commit, or URL; for the Site, the page URL.
4. Your **contact information** (address, telephone, email).
5. A statement that you have a **good-faith belief** the use is not authorized by the rights holder, its agent, or the law.
6. A statement, **under penalty of perjury**, that the information in your notice is accurate and that you are the rights holder or authorized to act on their behalf.

Incomplete notices may delay our response. Please do not send sensitive personal data beyond what is needed.

## 5. Our process after a valid notice

Upon receiving a valid notice, we will, as appropriate and within a reasonable time:

- review the claim in good faith;
- remove or disable access to the identified material on the Site or in repository assets we control;
- notify the contributor or uploader, where one is identifiable, and forward the notice;
- for content hosted on GitHub, you may also use **GitHub's DMCA process** (https://docs.github.com/articles/dmca-takedown-policy), which governs the repository host.

## 6. Counter-notice

If your material was removed and you believe this was a mistake or misidentification, you may send a counter-notice including: your signature; identification of the removed material and its prior location; a statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good-faith belief the removal was a mistake; your contact information; and your consent to jurisdiction as required by applicable law. If we receive a valid counter-notice and the original complainant does not pursue legal action within the period required by law, we may restore the material.
